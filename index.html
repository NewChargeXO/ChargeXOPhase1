Cell Technology

Anode (Graphite)

The anode (or negative electrode) in Lithium-ion battery is typically made up of Graphite, coated on Copper Foil. Graphite is a crystalline solid with a black/grey color and a metallic sheen. It’s a reducing electrode that releases electrons to the external circuit and oxidizes during the electrochemical reaction.

Electrolyte (Lithium Salts)

Electrolyte plays a key role in transporting the positive lithium ions between the cathode and anode. High purity electrolytes are a core component of li-ion batteries. The most commonly used electrolyte is comprised of lithium salt, such as LiPF6 in an organic solution.
